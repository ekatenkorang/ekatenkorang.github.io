Who am I ?

An electrical engineering graduate with a passion for securing emerging technology.

I completed my Bachelor's degree in Electrical/Electronic Engineering in 2022 from the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (K.N.U.S.T). I am an aspiring research who has worked on various research projects spanning applied machine learning, wireless network efficiency, power system anomaly detection and networking paradigms. I am currently a Cybersecurity Research Assistant at the University Information Technology Services centre, K.N.U.S.T, where I explore applicable open-source security solutions.

Beyond my professional endeavors, I love participating in initiatives promoting diversity and empowerment. I am actively involved in BecauseSheCan, a volunteer organization which aims to bridge the gender gap in STEM, and the National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE), a global network of black engineers aimed at increasing the number of culturally responsible black engineers. I engaged in several fulfilling outreaches where I shared my knowledge to help these young minds navigate their educational journeys. I love challenges, and I approach challenges with fresh perspectives and embrace new ideas fearlessly.

Transformer Inter-turn Fault Detection using Artificial Intelligence

By: Edwin Tenkorang, Prof. E. A. Frimpong, Dr. Elvis Twumasi

January 2022 - April 2023

We explored the vulnerability of transformers to inter-turn faults, which can disrupt power supply, damage equipment, and endanger personnel. Read about groundbreaking project that developed an innovative method for early detection and precise localization, aiming to safeguard power systems from extensive damage.
